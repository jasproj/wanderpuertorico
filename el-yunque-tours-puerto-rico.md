El Yunque is the only tropical rainforest in the U.S. National Forest system — and it's just 45 minutes from San Juan. Towering trees dripping with epiphytes, hidden waterfalls, natural swimming holes, and the chance to spot Puerto Rican parrots make this a must-do day trip.

## Pro Tips for El Yunque

1. **Go early** — Afternoon clouds roll in by 2pm most days. Morning = better views, fewer crowds.
2. **Bring water shoes** — Trail crossings and waterfall pools have slippery rocks.
3. **Pack layers** — It's cooler up there (70s) and rain is always possible.
4. **Bug spray** — Mosquitoes love the rainforest. Reef-safe sprays work fine.
5. **Waterproof your phone** — You'll want photos at the falls.

---

## Best Time to Visit El Yunque

**Dry season (December–April):** Fewer rain showers, clearer skies, but more tourists.

**Green season (May–November):** Lusher forest, fuller waterfalls, smaller crowds, occasional heavy rain.

The forest is beautiful year-round — it's a *rain*forest, after all.
