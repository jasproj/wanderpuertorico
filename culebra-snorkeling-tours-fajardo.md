Culebra Island sits 17 miles off Puerto Rico's east coast—and its crystal-clear waters, untouched coral reefs, and resident sea turtle population make it the best snorkeling destination in the Caribbean. Flamenco Beach consistently ranks among the world's top beaches, but the real magic happens beneath the surface.

Day trips from Fajardo let you experience Culebra without the hassle of overnight logistics. Most tours include ferry tickets or fast boat transfers, snorkel gear, lunch, and multiple swim stops. Here's how to choose the right one.

## Pro Tips for Culebra Snorkeling

1. **Book the fast boat:** The public ferry is cheap but unreliable. Delays happen. Powerboat tours cost more but guarantee your day.
2. **Bring reef-safe sunscreen:** Culebra's reefs are healthy because visitors protect them. Chemical sunscreens damage coral.
3. **Sea turtles love mornings:** The first snorkel stop usually has the best turtle sightings before crowds arrive.
4. **Pack a dry bag:** Your phone, wallet, and clothes need protection during boat transfers.
5. **Don't skip Tamarindo:** Flamenco gets the fame, but Tamarindo Beach offers better snorkeling with less competition for space.
